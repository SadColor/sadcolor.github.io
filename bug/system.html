{{ topL }}Mobility options

Dash: Double-tap {{ d6 }} to start running forwards. You'll run as long as you hold forward. You can cancel a run into a crouch or block, or you can do dashing normals by pressing {{ B }} or {{ C }} during a run.
Dash normals can't be chained into other normals, but can still cancel into Skills.

Backstep: Double-tap {{ d4 }} to quickly step a short distance backwards. There is a small amount of invincibility at the start of a backstep.

Hop: Press {{ d6 }}{{ D }} to perform a short jump forwards. It leits you perform aerial attacks faster than a normal jump, but doesn't avoid grounded attacks as well. You can't chain or special cancel moves from a hop.

Super Jump: Quickly press down and then a jumping direction to perform a larger jump upwards.

{{ topL }}Super Block

You can press {{ S }} while holding {{ d7 }}, {{ d4 }}, or {{ d1 }} to perform a Super Block.
Successfully Super Blocking an attack will reduce blockstun and pushback, nullify chip damage, and give you extra SP and AP.
Additionally, Super Block is the only way to block some air-unblockable attacks.
Don't throw it out recklessly! Super Blocking locks you into an animation for a short period of time, leaving you vulnerable to throws or lows/overheads if you were blocking the wrong way.
Blocking an attack before you've fully recovered (but after the correct timing window) will also increase chip damage and blockstun, while leaving your opponent closer.

{{ topL }}Burst

When the Burst meter is full, you can press {{ A }}{{ B }}{{ C }}{{ S }} during hitstun or blockstun to interrupt and perform a burst that knocks the opponent away if it hits.
The Burst meter refills from empty to full in about 60 - 80 seconds. The refill rate increases as your health gets lower. A successful connect recovers 30% of your Burst meter.
Burst startup is only vulnerable to GRABs, and the recovery of a burst is a counterhit state.
