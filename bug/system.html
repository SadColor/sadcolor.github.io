{{ topL }}Mobility options

Dash
{{ nesttopL }} Double-tap {{ d6 }} to start running forwards. You'll run as long as you hold forward. You can cancel a run into a crouch or block, or you can do dashing normals by pressing {{ B }} or {{ C }} during a run.
Dash normals can't be chained into other normals, but can still cancel into Skills. {{ nestcornerR }}
Backstep
{{ nesttopL }} Double-tap {{ d4 }} to quickly step a short distance backwards. There is a small amount of invincibility at the start of a backstep. {{ nestcornerR }}
Hop
{{ nesttopL }} Press {{ d6 }}{{ D }} to perform a short jump forwards. It lets you perform aerial attacks faster than a normal jump, but doesn't avoid grounded attacks as well. You can't chain or special cancel moves from a hop. {{ nestcornerR }}
Super Jump
{{ nesttopL }} Quickly press down and then a jumping direction to perform a larger jump upwards. {{ nestcornerR }}

{{ topL }}Resources

Skill Points (SP)
{{ nesttopL }} Skill Points are used when performing the special moves (called Skills) that characters have.
You start every round of a match with a filled SP gauge. The SP gauge naturally refills quickly, but natural refill is paused for a second when you perform a Skill or when either player lands a hit on the other.
You also gain SP for hitting the opponent, blocking attacks, or getting hit. {{ nestcornerR }}
Active Gauge (AP)
{{ nesttopL }} The Active Gauge starts each round empty and slowly increases over time. It takes about 15 seconds to naturally build 1 AP, and you can hold up to 2 AP.
You slightly increase the Active Gauge from the same things that increase SP gauge, but also by simply moving towards the opponent.
AP is consumed when using Chain Cancel or Guard Cancel. {{ nestcornerR }}
Exceed Meter (EX)
{{ nesttopL }} The Exceed Meter starts each match empty, but carries over between rounds in the same match.
It also builds from the same things that build the SP gauge, but it doesn't increase on its own.
EX is consumed when using EX Skills. {{ nestcornerR }}
Burst Meter
{{ nesttopL }} The Burst Meter is used for, well... Bursts. More info about Bursts in the Defense section below.
The Burst Meter starts the match full, and carries over between rounds. The Burst meter takes about 80 seconds to refill normally, but the refill rate increases as your health gets lower.
 {{ nestcornerR }}

{{ topL }}Offense

Chaining
{{ nesttopL }} Unless otherwise stated, all normals and command normals can freely chain from one to another on hit.
In an unbroken chain, a particular normal can only be used once. Returning to a neutral state (standing, crouching, or jumping) will break a chain. {{ nestcornerR }}
Throws
{{ nesttopL }} Throws (or Grabs) are performed by pressing {{ A }}+{{ D }}. This is a quick, short-ranged attack that ignores blocking. However, normal throws can be broken (or teched) by the opponent if they input their own throw command within a short window of being grabbed.
For the most part, you cannot throw an opponent in the middle of a combo. Additionally, throwing someone who is currently in blockstun or has just exited blockstun will result in them having double the time to break the throw.
The defender will have the advantage if they break a throw. If both players throw each other on the same frame, they will be at a neutral position.
Another benefit of throws is that the opponent is unable to Burst out of a throw. If you can combo from your throw, then the next hit is guaranteed! {{ nestcornerR }}
EX Skills
{{ nesttopL }} Exceed Skills are more powerful Skills that cost 100 EX.
EX Skills freeze the screen for a short period when they activate. They also prevent the opponent from Bursting out of them on hit, so they're good to get guaranteed damage.
All EX Skills have a higher-than-normal minimum damage, making them especially good for extra damage at the end of combos. {{ nestcornerR }}
Chain Cancel
{{ nesttopL }} If you have 1 AP available, you can press {{ D }}+{{ S }} or double-tap {{ D }} to perform an Chain Cancel.
This will cancel any current action (including Skills and EX Skills) and leave you in a neutral state where you can do whatever you want.
When an Chain Cancel is performed, the screen will freeze for a short moment, allowing you to assess the situation. If the opponent is performing a move, a !! will appear next to them.
You can also interrupt the screen freeze early with any action besides walking or crouching.
Additionally, if done during a combo, the opponent will be slowed down for a short period of time, allowing more combos that aren't normally possible. {{ nestcornerR }}

{{ topL }}Defense

Recovery
{{ nesttopL }} When hitstun ends while you're knocked down or being juggled, you can perform a recovery (also known as a tech or ukemi). Even if you don't recover, you're still invincible until your character gets back up.
When downed, you can recover in place by holding {{ d2 }}, or backwards by holding {{ d4 }}. The neutral recovery is a little quicker, while the back recovery leaves you further away from your opponent.
In the air, you can recover forwards, backwards, neutral, or downwards. Forward, back, and neutral recovery pop you up in the air slightly while moving you in the direction you held, while downwards recovery moves you straight down. This lets you get to the ground faster, but unlike other forms of recovery, you're vulnerable for a short period of time once you land before you can act. {{ nestcornerR }}
Super Block
{{ nesttopL }} You can press {{ S }} while holding {{ d7 }}, {{ d4 }}, or {{ d1 }} to perform a Super Block.
Successfully Super Blocking an attack will reduce blockstun and pushback, nullify chip damage, and give you extra SP and AP.
Additionally, Super Block is the only way to block some air-unblockable attacks.
Don't throw it out recklessly! Super Blocking locks you into an animation for a short period of time, leaving you vulnerable to throws or lows/overheads if you were blocking the wrong way.
Blocking an attack before you've fully recovered (but after the correct timing window) will also have the reverse effect: increasing chip damage and blockstun, while leaving your opponent closer. {{ nestcornerR }}
Guard Cancel
{{ nesttopL }} While blocking, you can input {{ d6 }}{{ D }} to interrupt your blockstun and try to knock the opponent away for 1 AP.
If the attack whiffs, you'll be invincible for the rest of the animation. If it whiffs or is blocked, you're still punishable. {{ nestcornerR }}
Chain Cancel
{{ nesttopL }} Chain Cancel can also be used defensively!
As mentioned earlier, the screen freezes when you use an Chain Cancel. This lets you take extra time to judge the situation and act accordingly.
The !! that appears when the opponent is performing an action also lets you counter with your own invincible move and escape their pressure. {{ nestcornerR }}
Burst
{{ nesttopL }} When the Burst Meter is full, you can press {{ A }}+{{ B }}+{{ C }}+{{ D }} during hitstun or blockstun to interrupt and try to blast the opponent away.
If it hits, the opponent will be sent all the way across the screen and you will recover 30% of your Burst Meter. Burst startup is only vulnerable to Grabs, and the recovery of a Burst is a counterhit state. If the opponent reads your Burst, they can pre-emptively grab you, or block it and get a big punish. {{ nestcornerR }}
