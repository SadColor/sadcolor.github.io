The character pages have lots consolidated info on each attack.
Here are explanations of various move attributes.

Cost
The resource that the move costs (whether it's SP, EX meter, or a character-specific one).
Blocking direction
Attacks are classified as lows, mids, or highs. Most attacks are mid attacks, which can be blocked standing or crouching.
Low attacks can only be blocked crouching.
High attacks can only be blocked standing. Every air normal is a high.
Grab
Grabs can't be blocked, but can be broken by inputting a grab at close to the same time you were grabbed.
Crush
Attacks that guard crush can't be blocked normally.
If they are, the opponent is put into a special vulnerable recoil state.
Super Block
Some attacks can only be blocked by Super Blocking them (abbreviated SB), in addition to their normal blocking direction.

Green attributes relate to how you can use the move.

Air OK
The move can also be done in the air.
Charge OK
When performing a Charge OK move, you can hold down the button to charge it up and get an enhanced version with altered properties.
Jump OK and Dash OK
When the attack hits the opponent, you can cancel into a jump or dash.
Every normal and command normal is Jump OK on hit unless otherwise noted.
Airborne
Airborne moves are initiated from the ground, but take place partially or entirely in the air.
Invincible
Invincible moves have a significant amount of invincibility, usually until they become active.
Cross-up
Cross-up attacks can switch sides with the opponent while correcting your facing direction.
Cross-up attacks must be blocked in the correct direction.
Ender
Enders force a combo to end when they hit, unless you use === or an EX Skill.
Note that almost every air throw is listed as an Ender, but you can still combo from them as long as they are the first move in a combo.

Red attributes relate to how they affect the opponent when they are hit.

Damage
Just about every attack damages the opponent when it connects, decreasing their HP.
Launch
Launch attacks always knock the opponent into the air.
Knockdown
Knockdown attacks always cause the opponent to fall to the ground, even if hitstun runs out and you can no longer hit them.
This is in contrast to Launch attacks, where the opponent can recover in mid-air if hitstun runs out.
Spike
Spike attacks send aerial opponents downwards.
There is no special effect on grounded opponents.
Groundbounce and Wallbounce
Bounce attacks will cause the opponent to rebound when they hit the floor or wall.
Groundslide
Groundslide attacks cause the opponent to slide across the ground.
Unlike a normal downed state, the opponent can be hit while sliding.
Wallsplat
Wallsplat attacks cause the opponent to stick to the wall, sort of similar to a Wallbounce.
Once the splat runs out, the opponent can aerial recover.
Crumple
During a Crumple, the opponent slowly falls to their knees then collapses to the floor.
While they're on their knees, they're considered grounded and even grabs (which normally can't combo) can connect.
After that, they're considered airborne.
Popup
Popup attacks cause the opponent to be briefly airborne, before landing and becoming grounded again.
Tumble
Tumble knock the opponent backwards off their feet. If you hit them during the tumble, they're considered crouching.
Capture
Capture attacks lock the opponent in place momentarily. Afterwards, they will Crumple on the ground or just fall straight down in the air.
Vacuum
Vacuum attacks move the opponent towards you rather than away.

Blue attributes deal with special status effects or damage scaling.
All status effects expire either after a fixed amount of time (based on the move that inflicted it) or if the person who caused the status is hit.

IP and CP
Combo Proration is always added to the Combo Count when the attack hits the opponent. Initial Proration is added on top of that when the move is the first attack in a combo.
% min.
Moves with a % minimum cannot deal below the listed percentage of their base damage, even if damage scaling should make it lower than that percentage.
Freeze
Frozen opponents will be encased in a block of ice. After a short moment (or if the opponent is hit) the ice will break.
If the opponent is on the ground when the ice breaks, they will Crumple. If they are in the air, then they'll instead be launched with a very slow fall speed.
Poison
When poisoned, a small amount of your health is converted into recoverable damage at regular intervals.
Burn
When burned, you will take an extra 10% unscaled damage from fire-based attacks.
Numb
When numbed, your speed is decreased, so you move and attack more slowly.
Ink
When inked, you are unable to use any Skills.
Heal
When the attack connects, you recover HP.
